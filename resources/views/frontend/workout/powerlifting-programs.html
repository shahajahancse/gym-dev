Powerlifting Programs

The Power Hypertrophy Upper Lower (PHUL) workout program is designed to combine elements of strength training (power) with muscle-building (hypertrophy) within an upper-lower body split. This program is ideal for individuals looking to build both strength and muscle mass, as it balances heavy, low-rep strength work with lighter, higher-rep hypertrophy work.


Program Overview

Frequency: Typically 4 days per week (2 upper body days, 2 lower body days).


Structure:



  2. Power Days: Focus on strength with low-rep, heavy-weight exercises.
  3. Hypertrophy Days: Focus on muscle growth with moderate to high-rep, moderate-weight exercises.


Sample PHUL Program
Upper Body Power


  • Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
  • Exercises:

    2. Bench Press: 4 sets of 4-6 reps
    3. Bent-Over Rows: 4 sets of 4-6 reps
    4. Overhead Press: 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    5. Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    6. Barbell Curls: 3 sets of 6-8 reps
    7. Tricep Dips or Skull Crushers: 3 sets of 6-8 reps


  • Cool-Down: Static stretching for upper body muscles.


Lower Body Power


  • Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
  • Exercises:

    2. Squats: 4 sets of 4-6 reps
    3. Deadlifts: 4 sets of 4-6 reps
    4. Leg Press: 3 sets of 6-8 reps
    5. Hamstring Curls: 3 sets of 6-8 reps
    6. Calf Raises: 4 sets of 8-12 reps


  • Cool-Down: Static stretching for lower body muscles.


Upper Body Hypertrophy


  • Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
  • Exercises:

    2. Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
    3. Seated Cable Rows: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
    4. Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
    5. Face Pulls: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
    6. Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
    7. Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 10-15 reps


  • Cool-Down: Static stretching for upper body muscles.


Lower Body Hypertrophy


  • Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
  • Exercises:

    2. Front Squats or Goblet Squats: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
    3. Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
    4. Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
    5. Leg Curls: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
    6. Standing Calf Raises: 4 sets of 12-20 reps


  • Cool-Down: Static stretching for lower body muscles.


Key Principles


  2. Progressive Overload: Gradually increase the weights or resistance to continue making progress in both strength and muscle size.
  3. Form and Technique: Focus on proper form to maximize effectiveness and reduce the risk of injury.
  4. Rest and Recovery: Allow for adequate recovery between workouts and ensure proper sleep to support muscle growth and repair.
  5. Nutrition: Maintain a balanced diet with sufficient protein, carbohydrates, and fats to support both strength gains and muscle hypertrophy.


Tips for Success


  • Warm-Up and Cool-Down: Always include a proper warm-up and cool-down to prepare your body for exercise and aid recovery.
  • Variety: Change up exercises, angles, or equipment every 6-8 weeks to prevent plateaus and keep workouts engaging.
  • Listen to Your Body: Adjust weights, reps, and sets based on how you feel and your progress.
